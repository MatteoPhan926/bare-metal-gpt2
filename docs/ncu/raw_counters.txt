WHAT THE PUBLISHED FIGURES ARE, DERIVED FROM THE ABOVE
==============================================================================================

  sectors / request  = l1tex__t_sectors_pipe_lsu_mem_global_op_ld.sum
                     / l1tex__t_requests_pipe_lsu_mem_global_op_ld.sum
  global-load eff.   = smsp__sass_average_data_bytes_per_sector_mem_global_op_ld.ratio / 32

                                  sec/req   bytes/sector      eff     DRAM read      duration
  k_gemv_fp16   head                3.333     32.000 B    100.00%     77.20 MB      317.28 us
  k_matmul      qkv  (naive)       16.491      2.002 B      6.26%      3.55 MB      109.34 us
  k_matmul_tiled qkv                1.887     16.495 B     51.55%      3.55 MB      100.32 us
  k_matmul      head (naive)       16.487      2.002 B      6.26%     77.30 MB      962.72 us
  k_matmul_tiled head               1.887     16.492 B     51.54%     77.30 MB     1927.52 us

  Naive's 2.002 bytes per 32-byte sector is EXACTLY ONE fp16 per sector: its W stride is K*2 = 1536 B,
  far wider than a sector, so every lane's load lands in a sector of its own. k_gemv_fp16 uses __half2
  vector loads (32 lanes x 4 B = 128 B = 4 sectors/request ideal) and hits 100%.

  THE HEAD SHAPE IS WHERE TILING LOSES. Both head kernels read the same 77.30 MB from DRAM, yet the
  tiled one takes 2.00x as long (1927.52 vs 962.72 us): the 16x16 tile computes 16 output rows and
  masks 15 at the write, burning 16x the FLOPs. Coalescing wins the transaction argument (1.887 vs
  16.487 sectors/request) and still loses the wall clock. This is why Stage 5 ships a dedicated M=1
  GEMV, and why the tied head DRAGS the naive->tiled M=1 decode ratio down rather than amplifying it.

  IMPLIED DRAM PEAK, five independent kernels (read GB/s / read pct_of_peak):
    243.330711 / 0.95137417 = 255.8      80.297690 / 0.31396549 = 255.8
     32.485806 / 0.12704640 = 255.7      40.104989 / 0.15680068 = 255.8
     35.379904 / 0.13842352 = 255.6
  ≈ the 256 GB/s theoretical peak (128-bit bus @ 16 Gbps). Divide by the READ-ONLY pct_of_peak, not by
  the Speed-of-Light "DRAM Throughput" (96.27% for the head GEMV), which folds in writes.
